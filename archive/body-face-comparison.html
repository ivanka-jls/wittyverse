Premise

Body face for the brand chrome layer

Seven candidates rendered with the same brand-facing 180-word paragraph + UI samples + a BSD pairing. Read in each face below and pick by feel — body type is the layer the eye spends 80% of its time in, so this decision quietly carries a lot of weight.

Scope: This decision is for the brand chrome layer only — homepage, storefront, product/marketing pages, brand-guidelines, brand-one-pager, brand-bible header. Winston's notebook / diary / in-universe long-form content will have its own typography treatment defined separately. So this comparison is about chrome body, not editorial body.

My read

What I'd actually do

The shortlist narrows to three: Public Sans, Hanken Grotesk, General Sans.

With seven on the table, the field separates into clear brackets. Inter (current) and Plus Jakarta are the conservative bracket — fine but contribute nothing to brand voice. Space Grotesk has the most distinctive letterforms but its tech-grotesque vibe pulls toward crypto/SaaS, not candy-bar/showman. DM Sans is the warmest but loses some UI legibility at small sizes. That leaves Public Sans, Hanken Grotesk, General Sans as the three genuine candidates — and each one matches a different facet of the brand's voice.

Public Sans — the civic / paperwork pick. The most thematically aligned option for Wittyverse specifically. Winston's brand pretends to be a legitimate authorized importer — the mono CYCLE 247 tags, the regulatory microline, "Operated by Winston, Dr. W. Yeti PhD," the customs-form aesthetic — all lean into deadpan-bureaucratic credibility. Public Sans (literally designed by the US government for federal websites) extends that joke into the body type itself. Body voice that reads as "we have our paperwork in order." Excellent UI legibility (designed for accessibility), excellent numerics, low risk. Slightly less distinctive than Hanken or General Sans, but distinctiveness isn't the question — register-fit is, and Public Sans hits a register the others don't.

Hanken Grotesk — the editorial pick. Older-feeling humanist character, has opinions about letterforms, leans toward the Onion / pulp-magazine / editorial register the bible references. Best if the brand should feel like "premium craft, slightly weathered."

General Sans — the premium pick. Contemporary humanist with cleaner geometry, Vercel-adjacent but warmer. Reads as "premium DTC brand that takes its packaging seriously" — Liquid Death adjacent. Best if the brand should feel like "premium craft, freshly printed." Loads from Fontshare instead of Google Fonts, so one fewer mass-defaulted face in your stack.

How to choose:

→ Read in each of the three samples for 30 seconds. Then ask: which brand-voice cue do you want body type to quietly reinforce?

→ "We have proper paperwork and authorized portal access" → Public Sans.

→ "We are a serious editorial publication that happens to import from a parallel dimension" → Hanken Grotesk.

→ "We are a premium DTC brand whose product happens to be portal-sourced" → General Sans.

My narrow lean — revised: Public Sans. The "authorized importer / customs-broker" framing is the strongest joke the brand already runs (it's why the mono tags exist, why "CYCLE 247" gets bureaucratic weight, why the regulatory microline is canon). Body type that reads as a federal-form face quietly extends that joke into every page — the body itself becomes part of the deadpan-authority bit. Hanken and General Sans both make body MORE editorial / premium; Public Sans makes body more institutionally trustworthy, which is the funnier register for this specific brand.

If you want bolder: DM Sans is the warmest. Pick it if you're confident Wittyverse should read as "handmade by a snake-oil salesman" more than "premium importer storefront."
