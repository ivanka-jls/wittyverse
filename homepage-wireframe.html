§5 — Best Sellers · concrete product surface

Function: surface top-converting products as a direct shopping path. Pairs with the abstract gift-situation tiles in §4 by making the catalog concrete — browse abstractly (§4) → see concrete examples (§5) → read what others said (§6).

Small "CYCLE 247" pill next to the heading carries the foreign fingerprint without overstating it. Cycle is a real operational stamp, not decorative — passes §7.5 lore-discipline as standard UI affordance (a labeled section + a tag).

Sort logic naturally ties to dispatch operations — "best sellers" can be framed as "most-dispatched this cycle" in copy without needing a separate section.

Tile labels and product names can carry foreign fingerprint where natural; no special section-level WV mechanic needed beyond the Cycle pill.

Decisions to make:

• Sort window — this Cycle's bestsellers (rotating weekly, ties to dispatch rhythm) vs. evergreen all-time bestsellers vs. mixed (e.g., 3 current + 1 anchor). Tentative: this Cycle's bestsellers, rotates per dispatch.

• Tile count — 4 (locked default) vs. 6 with horizontal carousel vs. "Top 5" with numbered ranking.

• Quick-add behavior — instant add-to-cart vs. open variant chooser modal. Defer to product reality; if most bestsellers have variants, modal is safer.

• Heading variant — "Best sellers" (locked) vs. light flavor options ("Most-dispatched this cycle" / "What's leaving the dock fastest") deferred.

Mobile: 4-up → 2-up grid (matches §4 Browse Grid mobile pattern). Heading + pill stay on one line; if narrow, pill drops to a new line under the heading.

§10 — Discovery / Leak Mechanic · non-rendering pattern, sitewide

Function: A site-wide discovery pattern. As users explore (scroll, hover, return visits, time on page), small "leaks" of Wittyverse info appear — popup artifacts, margin notes, transmissions, photos or short clips of products in use on the other side. Reinforces the Depth Stack (progressive reveal across visits).

Where it surfaces on the homepage: hover-triggered margin notes throughout, an optional scroll-milestone popup (see §7 / §3 leak slot example), return-visit unlocks of additional leaks.

Lore reason: as users explore, they "strain the portal" and more Wittyverse info leaks through. Each leak is dated by Cycle and stamped by source institution (O.O.P.S., Patent Office, depot, etc.).

Pairs with the lore-justification principle in website-brief.md §7.5 — every leak must have a lore reason; decorative glitches are banned.

Tone discipline: leaks feel like "postcards arriving," not spooky/creepy. Warm, slightly mysterious, never horror-adjacent.

AI-generated video is a natural fit for richer leaks (short clips of Wittyverse-side product use).

Canonical visible instance: the §6 Transmission Feed. Lore-justified by portal leakage. Presentation-justified by Winston's MySpace-era design taste (he'd reach for a guestbook because that's what felt welcoming when he learned the web).

Same feed renders in two surfaces: §6 hero (4–5 entries, primary), and a small sidewide ambient widget (~3 entries, example shown above). Mixed Earth-side + Wittyverse-side voices flow through both.

Other leak forms (margin notes, hover popups, return-visit unlocks) are secondary to the feed but follow the same lore + tone discipline: postcard-style, never spooky.

Architecture notes (not on-page)

The Earth-side / Wittyverse-side toggle is not surfaced on the homepage in this hybrid. The IA still needs to accommodate it — most likely it lives on the About page or appears as a subtle "side door" element accessible from the masthead. The website brief requires the architecture to support the toggle even when it's not foregrounded.

Voice register split applies sitewide. Homepage default surfaces (hero, browse tiles, social proof, footer) read in third-person — the shop describes itself. About + service surfaces (404, cart, refund emails, order confirmations) read in first-person Winston.

Mobile collapse behavior is noted per section in each block. The wireframe at 1100px collapses the section grid to 1-col (comment stacks below render); at 900px and below collapses the hero, browse grid, counter layout, newsletter band, footer grid, and feed entries.

What this wireframe is for: evaluating the homepage's section list + each section's function + the unique-Wittyverse features it carries. Visual decisions (palette, type, motion, illustration vs photo, layout fidelity) are deliberately deferred.
