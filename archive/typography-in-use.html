Premise

Five faces, three compositions

Three live compositions below show the canon in action — a homepage hero (where Bowlby earns its one-per-page use), an article page (BSD-only territory, no Bowlby), and a social ad (a single dramatic moment). Annotations on the right of each frame name what's what and why.

What's what · why

  • 1
    Lilita OneThe wordmark. ALL CAPS, skewX(-6°), orange + ink + lime stack. Smaller in the hero than in the hero-banner — it's the brand mark, not the loud line.
  • 2
    JetBrains MonoThe deep-canon tag in the top-right. Lets R1 (in-universe canon) peek through the R2 storefront chrome.
  • 3
    Bowlby OneThe single loudest line on the page. Pulp-magazine-cover register, skewX(-7°), same orange + ink + lime stack as the wordmark for poster cohesion. One use per page — this is it.
  • 4
    BSD 900The subhead. Cream on chrome, skewX(-6°). Sits as the supporting headline under the Bowlby moment — same poster family, just smaller and quieter.
  • 5
    Inter 400-500The body copy. 14-16px, sentence case, line-height 1.55. Does the reading; never tries to be the headline.
  • 6
    BSD 900The CTA. Same display face as the subhead, just smaller and packed into a button. Lime on ink for the CTA-canon pairing.
Rule: Bowlby is allowed here because the hero is the brand's single loudest spread. If you add a second Bowlby line further down the page, the first one stops landing.
§2 · Article / long-form page

Where Bowlby never appears

A product page, blog post, doc, or checkout. These surfaces exist for reading, not for poster moments. BSD does all display work; Inter does the body; Bowlby is consciously absent. If you add Bowlby here you cheapen its use on the surfaces where it actually earns its keep.

How yeti-tracking actually works

Six instruments, one field journal, three months of testing. Cycle 247 wasn't the first portal expedition we ran — it was just the first one where the instruments came back legible.

Caveats and edge cases

The kit does not work below 4,000m. The kit does not work after dark. The kit does not work when the portal is closed (please check the cycle calendar). For all other cases, see the troubleshooting appendix at the back.

What's what · why

  • 1
    Inter 800 eyebrowThe "FIELD GUIDE · ISSUE 12" label. 11px uppercase letter-spaced 0.14em. Standard Inter eyebrow treatment.
  • 2
    BSD 900 H1The page headline. Skew -6°, ink on cream. This is the loudest line the article gets — and it's only as loud as BSD allows, which is just right for reading-focused surfaces.
  • 3
    Inter 400-500Body copy. Sentence case, italic only for emphasis (em / cite). Never set the body in the display face.
  • 4
    BSD 900 H2Section header. Skew -6°, purple-700. Same face as H1, smaller, different colour to establish hierarchy.
  • 5
    BSD 800 H3Sub-section header. Skew -5°, orange-700. Lighter weight (800 instead of 900) so it sits visually under H2.
Rule: Long-form pages have headings, not banners. Bowlby would feel like a billboard glued onto a magazine spread — it'd cheapen both the article and the next Bowlby moment.

Rule shorthand

  • If the line needs to be the brand's loudest moment on this surface → --font-pulp (Bowlby One). One per page max.
  • If the line is display copy but not THE moment → --font-display (Big Shoulders Display). Subheads, section heads, card titles, CTAs, eyebrows.
  • If the line is the wordmark or W monogram → --font-accent (Lilita One). Nothing else.
  • If the line is the regulatory tag or CYCLE 247 microline → --font-mono (JetBrains Mono). Nothing else.
  • Everything else → --font-body (Inter, for now — see body-face reconsideration in flight).
